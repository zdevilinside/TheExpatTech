The Amazing SR-71

ZDevilInside published on 2025-11-01 included in Aviation

The Blackbird

Many people do not know this but there were 2 aircraft named the Blackbird. The first, the A-12 was built by the CIA, was a single seat reconnaissance airplane. It was designated the A-12 because it was the 12 in a series of designs for the “Archangel” program. Archangel was the aircraft’s code name.
Lockheed and Convair were in competition to build a replacement for the U-2. They were trying to lower the radar cross section of the U-2 because it was so easily detected by the Soviet Union. At first, the CIA preferred the Convair Kingfisher program but the A-12’s specifications were a little better and its cost was much lower. Further, the companies track records were a factor; Convair had built the B-58 Hustler that had been plagued by delays and cost overruns. Lockheed had produced the U-2 on time and under budget.
The A-12 was built between 1962 and 1964. It flew from 1963 until 1968. The plane was the precursor to the SR-71 Blackbird, the M-21 launcher for the D-21 drone, and the YF-12 prototype. Its was originally designed to fly over Cuba and the Soviet Union but that plan was tossed away after Gary Powers was shot down.

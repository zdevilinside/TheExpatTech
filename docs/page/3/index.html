The Blackbird

Many people do not know this but there were 2 aircraft named the Blackbird. The first, the A-12 was built by the CIA, was a single seat reconnaissance airplane. It was designated the A-12 because it was the 12 in a series of designs for the “Archangel” program. Archangel was the aircraft’s code name.
Lockheed and Convair were in competition to build a replacement for the U-2. They were trying to lower the radar cross section of the U-2 because it was so easily detected by the Soviet Union. At first, the CIA preferred the Convair Kingfisher program but the A-12’s specifications were a little better and its cost was much lower. Further, the companies track records were a factor; Convair had built the B-58 Hustler that had been plagued by delays and cost overruns. Lockheed had produced the U-2 on time and under budget.
The A-12 was built between 1962 and 1964. It flew from 1963 until 1968. The plane was the precursor to the SR-71 Blackbird, the M-21 launcher for the D-21 drone, and the YF-12 prototype. Its was originally designed to fly over Cuba and the Soviet Union but that plan was tossed away after Gary Powers was shot down.

✈️ Blackbird Family Comparison: A-12, YF-12, and SR-71

Lockheed’s Skunk Works developed a family of high-speed reconnaissance and interceptor aircraft in the 1960s.
The A‑12, YF‑12, and SR‑71 Blackbird share a common lineage but differ in mission, configuration, and performance.

A-12

The A-12 was the clandestine forerunner of the SR-71 Blackbird. The airframes are nearly the same shape and have similar dimensions. While the SR-71 holds many records for a manned jet aircraft, the A-12 wflew higher and faster than the its sibling because its existence was classified for over 20 years.

Qualifying for Legal Residency in Mexico

ZDevilInside published on 2015-08-01 included in MovingToMexico

Since 2016, Mexico has increased the financial requirements to qualify for residency visas significantly. They used the country’s Daily Minimum Wage to calculate the minimum monthly incomes for foreigners that wish to live legally in Mexico as a temporary or permanent resident.

Mexico’s daily minimum wage has gone up over 3x what it was since 2016. That means that monthly incomes or savings/investments have increased along with those increases. The minimum wage in Mexico was 73 pesos/day in 2016. It is now 280 pesos a day.
